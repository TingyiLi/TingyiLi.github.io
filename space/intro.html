Lazy Cancer

“The bridge between reality and a dream is work.”

― Jared Leto

Lazy cancer is the disease you say you have when you procrastinate.

I am 22 and this is a disease I have for over 20 years.

In my conclusion, the reason is that you don't have enough passion for things you procrastinate.

(This is where she starts to deviate from main topic and become an annoying critic.)

There goes a saying in Chinese, "We are not working hard enough to compete by our talents." Chinese people work extremely hard, at least from my point of view. In Shanghai, people rush their life under the extreme weather so hard that they barely have time to take a breath and look around contemplating. Thus most of them actually don't even know what they aim for. They simply just do what most people do or what others told them to. A lot of people coming from outside the city chose to stay here with slightly higher salary and astonishing price of rent, only for the gorgeous city landscape and fabulous city life which never belonged and will never belong to them.

However, not all people are struggling life here in Shanghai. There are many rich, smart, gorgeous people out there living a fancy life here in Shanghai. They still work hard, but in a decent way, they may catch a morning rush in Bentley while you are on the subway.

Told by my happiness course instructor Amalia Jiva(Love her!!!), working hard is necessary for one to become truly happy. Working hard is not the point here. But it's just that when you are not aiming for success and money here in Shanghai, suddenly you are the weirdo. It's like when you are 25 and still single, people think there must be something wrong with you and you should quickly find someone to get married so that your life work is done.

The "age stereotype" is so harsh in China.

If you are still in school when you are 30, then you are probably a fool. If you are not married in you thirties, you are called "the leftovers". If you give up your manager position because you don't feel happy doing this job and you wanna change one, you are definitely an idiot.

Not just age, but....what is the right thing.

Like, if you study arts, people think you must suck at your schoolwork. If you play computer games and aim to become a professonal star, you are ready to be abandoned from your home. Etc.

I believe we are the changing generation. But we still need to face the reality, before dreams, we need to make a living.

I suddenly feel neccessary to express my gratefulness to my open-minded parents. Love you!

One thing I love about Sweden. No body cares about what you do. That's awesome.

Well, but in general, I had a great time in Shanghai. Can't deny, it's a great place to have fun and meet interesting people, taking challenges and enriching your life. And the food is cheap and fruits are juicy.

No matter what, it's time to say goodbye, magic city ^3^. Gotta miss you.

Lately my friends around me are talking a lot about relationships. One of them just got married. One of them started to use TanTan(Chinese Tinder). One of them broke up with her boyfriend and couldn't get over with him. One of them became gay. Many of them haven't even had a relationship. And yes, they are all the same age with me.

People from my age never feel bored talking about love, which is one sign of lack of experiences for life, in my opinions. Everybody is so devoted to love that they seem to be prepared to be another Jack and Rose, Romeo and Julie. So young, so brave, until they get hurt enough.

Chinese people are so easy to get hurt in relationships. In China, parents always forbid you from being in a relationship or even love someone before college and require you to get married after college. How can we accomplish that?! So we don't listen to them.

Conflicts between previous generation and temporary generation of opinions about relationships are extremely tense. And it's so easy to be immoral and disloyal in a relationship in China. Hard to explain.

At the same time, ridiculously, choosing to be single is also not recognizable because you look pathetic being alone doing what you like, honey.

I like "Song of Joy", a chinese TV series which is called the Chinese version of Sex and City. But it's still talking about things like Virgin Complex when it comes to relationships. Me: ? ? ?
